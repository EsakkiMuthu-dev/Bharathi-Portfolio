UX Case - I-Grow: Mobile Support App - Empowering Mental Wellness

  • by:Bharathi Ravi
  • date:January, 2024
  • UX, UI, Research, priorization,Mental health
This is an individual project that I made during the UX-UI study. It's designed to aid individuals seeking improved mental health.

Objective

To develop a mobile support app aimed at connecting lonely and depressed individuals with real persons or psychologists to provide support and alleviate feelings of loneliness and anxiety.

User Persona

  • Name: [Anonymous]
  • Age: Varies

Needs

  1. Connection:

    Users seek human connection and support to combat feelings of loneliness and depression.

  2. Accessibility:

    Users want access to support wherever they are, without the need to physically visit a counselor's office.

  3. Anonymity:

    Users may prefer anonymity when seeking support, allowing them to open up more freely about their feelings.

  4. Professional Support:

    Users desire access to qualified professionals who can effectively address their mental health concerns.


Problem Statement

Despite the interconnectedness provided by smartphones and social media, many individuals still experience profound feelings of loneliness and disconnection, leading to increased anxiety and depression.

Solution

Develop a mobile support app that facilitates connections between lonely individuals and qualified professionals or supportive peers. Ensure ease of use, accessibility, and anonymity to encourage users to seek help and support.

Ideas from user research

After connecting with several users, I discovered significant insights that informed the direction of the app:

1. Need for Meaningful Connection: Users expressed dissatisfaction with existing social media platforms, which often prioritize short-term entertainment content and lack genuine connections. The prevalence of fake comments and negativity further exacerbates feelings of loneliness and depression.

"My friend, shared his desire to open up about personal matters he's never discussed with anyone. Privacy is paramount for him; he wants only volunteers to have access to his information." Esakki Muthu, 23.
"My sister, emphasized the importance of a distraction-free platform focused on connecting with professionals for support. She values simplicity and direct access to support resources." Poorna Ravi, 21.

2 -Preference for Voice Support: Some users expressed discomfort with expressing themselves through chat, highlighting the need for call support within the app.

"Other users mentioned their discomfort with chat-based communication and expressed a preference for call support."

These insights underscore the importance of creating a platform that prioritizes genuine connections, user privacy, and easy access to support resources. By addressing these needs, we aim to empower individuals to seek the support they need in times of distress.

Avoiding featurism and prioritizing user needs

From my research, I found that privacy is a major concern for individuals when sharing personal information that they don't disclose to others, as highlighted by my friend Esakki Muthu. Additionally, our app should strive to minimize distractions, as mentioned by my sister. Therefore, it's crucial to avoid unnecessary features to maintain a distraction-free environment.

Furthermore, not everyone is comfortable with chat support, so incorporating call and group call support is essential to cater to diverse user preferences. By prioritizing user needs over feature overload, we ensure a seamless and user-friendly experience for all.

Implementation

  1. Research:

    Conducted research on loneliness and mental health issues prevalent in modern society, identifying the need for accessible support platforms.

  2. Design:

    Developed wireframes and prototypes for the mobile support app, focusing on intuitive navigation and user-friendly interface design.

  3. Development:

    Synthesized insights from various online resources to design and implement features such as chat and call functionalities with real persons or psychologists

Upcoming Features

  • Currently, the focus has been on creating a simple workflow from the user's perspective, facilitating joining and communication with volunteers through voice calls and chat. Additionally, users have the option to connect in group calls for added support and community interaction.
  • In the near future, plans are underway to incorporate features that will allow volunteers to register and participate in the app. This will include:
    • Developing the flow for volunteers to connect with individuals seeking support.
    • Enabling one-on-one interactions to provide personalized assistance and guidance.

Stay tuned for updates as we continue to enhance the platform for a more comprehensive support experience.

Lessons Learned

  1. The importance of addressing mental health concerns in the digital age and providing accessible support platforms for those in need.
  2. The value of anonymity in encouraging users to seek help and support for their mental health issues.
  3. Continuous improvement through user feedback and iteration to optimize app usability and effectiveness in addressing user needs.

Conclusion

The development of the mobile support app provided a valuable solution to the pressing issue of loneliness and depression in modern society. By leveraging technology to facilitate connections between individuals and mental health professionals, the app has made significant strides in improving mental well-being and fostering a sense of community among users.
